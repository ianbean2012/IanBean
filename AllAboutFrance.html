A General Introduction to: France

France is a Western European country with a population of 67.39 million. It has a very rich culture, especially with food. There are a lot of special dishes that come from France, and French resteraunts are usually very fancy to give you the best authentic experience. Some of the trademark foods that comes from France include:

  • Baguettes
  • Crossaint
  • Wine
  • French Steak
  • Onion Soup

Another huge part of French dining is one that many Americans are familiar with-dessert! Many things Americans and people around the world enjoy actually comes from France (See how much of this list you can relate to!):

  • Macarones
  • Chocolate Waffles

There's very good food but it can cost a lot of money! The whole country is centered around the capital of Paris, which hold many of the nation's trademark sites such as the Eiffel Tower and the Louvre. Some things to see and do in France (mostly Paris) include:

  • The aforementioned Eiffel Tower and the Louvre
  • A cruise along the Seine river, running through Paris
  • Versaille Palace

Regarding travel, the biggest airport in France, unsurprisingly, is Paris's Charles De Gaulle international, followed by Paris Orly and then Nice International. The largest cities are:

  1. Paris
  2. Marseille
  3. Lyon
  4. Toulouse
  5. Nice
